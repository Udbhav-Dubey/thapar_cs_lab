If Saiyans are the gods of brute force, Batman is the god of **contingency**. While he occupies a world full of aliens and metahumans, his true "power" isn't his money—it's his refusal to be outworked or surprised.

### The Psychology of the Bat

Bruce Wayne’s greatest asset isn’t his Batarang; it’s his **weaponized trauma**. After witnessing the murder of his parents, he transformed his grief into a rigid moral code and a state of perpetual readiness.

* **The Mask:** Most superheroes wear a mask to protect their "real" lives. For Batman, the mask is the real person; "Bruce Wayne" is the performance he puts on for the public.
* **The No-Kill Rule:** This isn't just about morality; it's a mental safety valve. He knows that if he crosses that line once, his obsession would turn him into the very thing he fights.

### The "Prep Time" Mythos

The running joke in the DC fandom is that Batman can beat anyone with enough "prep time." This is based on his **Genius-Level Intellect** and **Tactical Mastery**:

* **The Utility Belt:** It’s a mobile lab. It contains everything from shark repellent (in the campy days) to **Kryptonite rings** and specialized EMPs.
* **Detective Skills:** Before he throws a punch, he’s already analyzed his opponent’s heart rate, fighting style, and psychological triggers. He doesn't fight fair; he fights to win before the battle even starts.

### The Rogues Gallery

Batman is defined by his villains, most of whom are dark reflections of his own psyche:

* **The Joker:** Pure chaos vs. Batman’s absolute order.
* **Two-Face:** The fragility of justice and the duality of man.
* **Scarecrow:** The mastery of fear—the very tool Batman uses on others.
