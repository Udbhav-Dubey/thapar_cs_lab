Superman originated as a fictional superhero created in the late 1930s during the Golden Age of comics.
He was created by writer Jerry Siegel and artist Joe Shuster, inspired by mythological heroes and pulp fiction.
The character first appeared in Action Comics in 1938 and quickly became a cultural icon.
Superman was born on the planet Krypton to parents Jor-El and Lara.
As Krypton faced destruction, he was sent to Earth as a baby in a small spaceship.
He was found and raised by the Kent family in Smallville, Kansas.
Growing up as Clark Kent, he learned to hide his extraordinary powers.
As an adult, he chose to use those powers to protect humanity and represent hope and justice.
